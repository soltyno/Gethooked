#gethooked

Approximately 120 billion farmed fish are killed yearly, meaning they are number 1 on the list of slaughtered animals, yet there are almost no regulations regarding the welfare of the fishes nor even discussions on this topic. You might ask why is it such a big issue? Well, there is more to the topic than you might think.

01.

Current situation

Fishes are either captured with methods that are extremely painful or are farmed in crowded and highly unnatural environment.

At the end they are usually gutted alive which results in slow and painful death.

02.

Fish can feel

In a book ‘Do Fish Feel Pain?’, the author, biologist Victoria Braithwaite says that “there is as much evidence that fish feel pain and suffer as there is for birds and mammals.”

They will not scream, but if you are willing to look closely, we can assure you that can see that.

03.

Fish can think

That’s right. Saying that fish have memory span of about 3 seconds is a myth. You can even teach them how to use tools or find objects by obtaining clues from landscape.
”

Some fish can even cooperate and communicate between each other in groups.

Almost all fishing methods inflict long- term pain and stress on fishes.

02.

When caught by trawling nets they are pursued to exhaustion and subsequently crushed under the weight of other fish;

03.

Fish suffer painful decompression of internal organs when raised from deep water;

04.

Fish caught on long lines can stay hooked, and dragged, for hours or even days.

Main problems

Worldwide

Some 1 trillion fishes are caught each year worldwide. The severity, duration and sheer number of animals suffering makes fishing a major animal welfare issue that must be addressed. There is an urgent need to implement fish stunning or more effective methods of fish killing immediately after catching.

Solutions

The most urgent and significant change would be an implementation of mandatory fish stunning. Effective and humane fish slaughter methods already exist. Fish stunning prior to slaughter is available both for small scale aquaculture farmers and fishermen as well as for big scale producers and fisheries operations.

What this petition stands for?

Demand humane methods of stunning before slaughter.

Regulate stocking densities to levels that provide better fish welfare

Regulate fish transport.

Invest in research focused on main farming species actual needs
